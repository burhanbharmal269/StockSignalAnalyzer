# StockSignalAnalyzer — Trading Platform Glossary

**Platform:** Institutional-Grade NSE F&O Trading Platform
**Audience:** Traders new to this platform or to algorithmic/systematic trading
**Last Updated:** June 2026

This glossary covers every term you will encounter in StockSignalAnalyzer. Terms are listed alphabetically. Where a term has a related concept, cross-references are noted in italics.

---

## A

**Access Token** — A temporary digital key that proves you are logged in to Kite (Zerodha's API). The platform uses it to place orders on your behalf. Access tokens expire daily at 06:00 IST, which is why you must reconnect to Kite every morning. Never share your access token with anyone.

**AI Insight** — A human-readable market analysis generated by Azure OpenAI. It combines price data, news sentiment, and technical indicators to produce a summary of current market conditions and potential risks. Insights appear on the AI Insights page and are updated throughout the trading day. See also: *Sentiment Score*.

**Audit Log** — A complete record of every action taken by the platform: signals generated, orders placed, risk rejections, kill switch activations, and user actions. Found in Settings. Useful for reviewing what happened and when, especially after an unusual trading session.

**Auto Kill Switch** — A feature where the risk engine automatically activates the Kill Switch without any user action. This happens when your daily loss limit or maximum drawdown threshold is breached. When the auto kill switch fires, you will receive an alert. You must manually review the situation and deactivate it when you are ready to resume trading. See also: *Kill Switch*, *Drawdown*.

---

## B

**Backtest** — Running a strategy against historical market data to see how it would have performed. Backtesting does not guarantee future results, but it reveals a strategy's characteristics — its win rate, typical drawdown, and return profile. Accessed from the Backtest page.

**Bollinger Bands** — A technical indicator consisting of three lines plotted around a price chart: a middle moving average and two outer bands representing two standard deviations above and below the average. When price moves outside the outer bands, it signals unusual volatility. The platform's Bollinger Breakout strategy uses these bands to detect breakout opportunities.

**Broker** — The entity that executes your orders at the exchange. In StockSignalAnalyzer, the broker for live trading is Zerodha (accessed through the Kite Connect API). In paper trading, the "broker" is a simulation engine inside the platform. See also: *Kite Connect*, *Paper Trading*.

---

## C

**Capital Allocation** — The portion of your total capital that you are willing to deploy in the market at any given time. Configured on the Capital page. For example, if you have ₹10,00,000 available and you set capital allocation to 50%, the platform will only deploy up to ₹5,00,000 across all positions.

**CE (Call Option)** — A financial contract that gives the buyer the right to purchase an underlying asset (like NIFTY) at a specific price (the strike price) before a specific date (the expiry). If you expect the market to rise, buying a CE can be profitable. "CE" stands for "Call European." See also: *Options*, *Strike Price*, *Expiry*.

**Confidence Score** — A number from 0 to 100 that represents how strongly a strategy believes in a particular signal. A score of 80 means the strategy's conditions are very clearly met. A score of 45 might indicate a marginal setup. You can configure a minimum confidence threshold in Settings — signals below this threshold will not generate orders.

**Correlation ID** — A unique reference code assigned to each signal, which is passed through to the order and position created from it. It lets you trace any order or position back to the signal that started it. Useful when reviewing why a specific order was placed.

---

## D

**Dead Man's Switch** — An alternative term sometimes used for the Kill Switch or Auto Kill Switch concept: a mechanism that triggers automatically if certain conditions are met (like a loss limit breach), without requiring manual action. In this platform, the Auto Kill Switch serves this purpose. See also: *Auto Kill Switch*, *Kill Switch*.

**Drawdown** — The decline in portfolio value from a previous peak. A 10% drawdown means your portfolio fell 10% from its highest point. Maximum drawdown is the worst such decline in a given period. The risk engine monitors drawdown and can trigger the Auto Kill Switch if it exceeds your configured maximum. A key indicator of strategy risk.

---

## E

**EMA (Exponential Moving Average)** — A moving average that gives more weight to recent prices than older ones, making it more responsive to new information. The platform uses EMA Crossover as one of its six strategies: when a shorter-term EMA crosses above a longer-term EMA (a "golden cross"), it generates a LONG signal; when it crosses below (a "death cross"), it generates a SHORT signal.

**Execution Analytics** — Statistics about how orders were executed, including latency (how fast), slippage (how close to the intended price), fill rates, and order rejection rates. Found on the Analytics page. See also: *Latency*, *Slippage*.

**Expiry** — The date on which an options or futures contract ends. For NSE instruments, expiry is typically the last Thursday of the month (for monthly contracts) or a specific Thursday each week (for weekly contracts). On expiry day, all open F&O positions in that contract are settled at the exchange-determined final price. You should close positions before expiry unless you intend to let them settle.

---

## F

**F&O (Futures & Options)** — The two main types of derivative instruments traded on the NSE. Futures are contracts to buy or sell an asset at a predetermined price on a future date. Options give the buyer the right (but not the obligation) to buy or sell at a predetermined price. StockSignalAnalyzer focuses exclusively on NSE F&O instruments.

**Futures** — A contract that obligates you to buy (if long) or sell (if short) a specific asset at a specific price on a specific future date. Unlike options, futures are binding on both parties. Example: A NIFTY futures contract (NIFTYFUT) represents an exposure to the NIFTY 50 index.

---

## K

**Kill Switch** — An emergency stop that immediately halts all new trading activity on the platform. When activated, no new orders are placed and pending orders are cancelled. Existing open positions are not closed automatically. The Kill Switch can be activated manually from the Dashboard or automatically by the risk engine. See also: *Auto Kill Switch*, *Dead Man's Switch*.

**Kite Connect** — Zerodha's official broker API (Application Programming Interface). It is the technology that allows StockSignalAnalyzer to place real orders in your Zerodha account. You must authenticate with Kite Connect every morning before live trading begins. See also: *Access Token*, *Session Expiry*.

---

## L

**Latency** — The time delay between when a signal is generated and when the corresponding order is placed. Measured in milliseconds. Lower latency means faster execution, which reduces the gap between your intended entry price and your actual fill price. Found in the Analytics page under execution statistics.

**Live Trading** — Trading with real money through your Zerodha broker account. Orders placed in live mode reach the NSE exchange and use real capital. Requires an active Kite Connect session. Contrast with *Paper Trading*.

**LONG** — A position or signal direction indicating an expectation that the price will rise. A LONG signal means buy the instrument. If you are "long NIFTY," you have bought NIFTY and profit if the price goes up.

**Lot Size** — The minimum quantity in which an F&O instrument can be traded, set by the exchange. For example, one lot of NIFTY futures represents 50 units of the index. You cannot trade half a lot — orders must be in whole lot multiples. Lot sizes are displayed on each instrument's detail page.

---

## M

**MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)** — A momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two exponential moving averages of a price. The MACD line crossing above a signal line suggests bullish momentum; crossing below suggests bearish momentum. The platform's MACD Divergence strategy looks for cases where price moves in one direction but MACD moves in the opposite direction — a divergence — which often precedes reversals.

**Market Breadth** — A measure of how many instruments in the market are participating in a move. If 400 out of 500 NIFTY 50 stocks are advancing, breadth is strong. If only 100 are advancing even though the index is up, breadth is weak. The Market Overview page shows breadth indicators. Weak breadth during a rally can be a warning sign.

---

## N

**NEUTRAL** — A signal direction indicating no strong directional view. When a strategy output is NEUTRAL, no order is generated. It means the strategy's conditions for either LONG or SHORT are not clearly met.

**NSE (National Stock Exchange)** — India's primary stock exchange, where StockSignalAnalyzer operates. All instruments tracked by the platform are traded on the NSE. NSE operates from 09:15 IST to 15:30 IST on trading days.

---

## O

**OI (Open Interest)** — The total number of outstanding options or futures contracts that have not been settled or closed. Rising OI alongside rising price generally confirms a bullish trend. Rising OI alongside falling price confirms a bearish trend. OI data is shown on the Market Overview and individual instrument pages.

**OMS (Order Management System)** — The internal component of StockSignalAnalyzer that receives validated signals from the risk engine and routes them to the appropriate broker (paper or live). The OMS tracks order state from creation through fill or cancellation. You interact with its output on the Orders page.

**Opportunities** — Early-stage setups identified by the Market Scanner that have not yet formed complete signals. Found on the Opportunities page. An opportunity might show an instrument approaching a key level that historically triggers one of the six strategies.

**Options** — Financial contracts that give the buyer the right (but not the obligation) to buy or sell an underlying asset at a specific price before a specific date. In NSE F&O markets, options are available on indices like NIFTY and BANKNIFTY, and on individual stocks. See also: *CE*, *PE*, *Strike Price*, *Expiry*.

---

## P

**Paper Trading** — Simulated trading using virtual money. No real orders reach the exchange. Everything works exactly like live trading — signals, orders, positions, and PnL — but no real capital is at risk. This is the default mode. Ideal for testing strategies and learning the platform.

**PCR (Put-Call Ratio)** — The ratio of traded Put options to Call options for a given instrument. A high PCR (more puts than calls) can indicate bearish sentiment; a low PCR (more calls than puts) can indicate bullish sentiment. Shown on the Market Overview page as a sentiment indicator.

**PE (Put Option)** — A financial contract that gives the buyer the right to sell an underlying asset at a specific price before a specific date. If you expect the market to fall, buying a PE can be profitable. "PE" stands for "Put European." See also: *Options*, *CE*.

**PnL (Profit and Loss)** — The financial result of a trade or portfolio, expressed in rupees. Positive PnL means profit; negative PnL means loss. The platform tracks PnL in two states: Unrealized (position still open) and Realized (position closed). See also: *Unrealized PnL*, *Realized PnL*.

**Portfolio** — A named grouping of positions. On the Portfolios page, you can create different portfolios to organize positions — for example, one portfolio for index strategies and another for stock-specific strategies. Portfolios help you analyze performance by strategy type or instrument group.

**Position** — An open trade. A position is created when an order is filled and remains open until an opposing order closes it. See the Positions page for all current open positions with their unrealized PnL.

---

## R

**Realized PnL** — The profit or loss that has been locked in by closing a position. Once a position is closed, the PnL becomes realized. In live trading, this translates to actual funds added to or removed from your account.

**Reconciliation** — The process of comparing the platform's internal records of positions and orders with the actual records at the broker (Zerodha). If there is a discrepancy (for example, an order shows as FILLED in the platform but PENDING at the broker), reconciliation flags it. This runs automatically and discrepancies appear in the System Health page.

**Regime** — The current character of the market. The platform detects three regimes:
- Trending — Price is moving steadily in one direction.
- Ranging — Price is moving sideways with no clear direction.
- Volatile — Price is moving rapidly in unpredictable directions.

Different strategies work better in different regimes. Momentum strategies like VWAP Momentum perform better in trending regimes. Mean reversion strategies like RSI Mean Reversion perform better in ranging regimes.

**Risk Engine** — The validation component that checks every signal before it becomes an order. It enforces your configured risk parameters: position size limits, capital limits, daily loss limits, and drawdown limits. If a signal fails any check, it is rejected and logged with a reason. See the Risk page to configure limits.

**Risk Profile** — A named set of risk parameters (position limits, loss limits, drawdown limits). You can save multiple profiles for different market conditions and switch between them on the Risk page. For example, you might use a "Conservative" profile during earnings season and a "Moderate" profile in quieter periods.

**RSI (Relative Strength Index)** — A momentum indicator measured from 0 to 100 that shows how overbought or oversold an instrument is. An RSI below 30 is generally considered oversold (potential buy setup). An RSI above 70 is generally considered overbought (potential sell setup). The platform's RSI Mean Reversion strategy uses these levels to generate signals.

---

## S

**Sentiment Score** — A numerical measure of market mood from -1 (very bearish) to +1 (very bullish). Calculated from news articles processed by Azure OpenAI and NewsAPI.ai. A score near 0 is neutral; strong positive or negative scores indicate clear market mood. Shown on the AI Insights and Market Overview pages.

**Session** — Your logged-in connection to Kite. Each session is authenticated with an access token that expires daily. You must start a new session each trading day by reconnecting through the Broker page.

**Session Expiry** — The daily expiration of your Kite session at 06:00 IST. After expiry, any attempt to place a live order will be rejected until you reconnect. The platform's SessionExpiryWatcher monitors this and alerts you in advance.

**Sharpe Ratio** — A measure of return per unit of risk. Calculated as the average return divided by the standard deviation of returns. A Sharpe Ratio above 1.0 is generally acceptable; above 2.0 is strong; above 3.0 is excellent. Shown in Analytics. A high Sharpe Ratio means you are earning good returns without taking excessive risk.

**SHORT** — A position or signal direction indicating an expectation that the price will fall. A SHORT signal means sell the instrument (open a short position). If you are "short BANKNIFTY," you have sold BANKNIFTY and profit if the price falls.

**Signal** — The platform's recommendation to trade a specific F&O instrument in a specific direction (LONG or SHORT). Generated by one of the six strategies after analyzing market data. Each signal includes entry price, stop loss, target, confidence score, and the generating strategy. Found on the Signals page.

**Slippage** — The difference between the price at which a signal was generated and the actual price at which the order was filled. For example, if a LONG signal on NIFTY was generated at 22,500 but the order filled at 22,510, slippage is 10 points. Some slippage is inevitable due to market movement between signal generation and order execution. Shown in Analytics.

**Stop Loss** — A predefined price level at which a losing trade is automatically closed to prevent further loss. Each signal generated by the platform includes a suggested stop loss. Example: A LONG signal at 22,500 might have a stop loss at 22,400, meaning if the price falls to 22,400, the position is closed with a 100-point loss.

**Strategy** — One of the six analysis methods built into the platform that generates trading signals. Each strategy uses different technical indicators and market conditions to identify opportunities. The six strategies are: RSI Mean Reversion, VWAP Momentum, Supertrend Crossover, Bollinger Breakout, MACD Divergence, and EMA Crossover.

**Strike Price** — The specific price at which an options contract gives the right to buy (for calls) or sell (for puts). Example: NIFTY 22500 CE gives the right to buy NIFTY at 22,500. The current market price relative to the strike price determines whether an option is "in the money," "at the money," or "out of the money."

**Supertrend** — A technical indicator that plots a line above or below price to indicate the current trend direction. When price is above the Supertrend line, the trend is bullish; when below, it is bearish. The platform's Supertrend Crossover strategy generates signals when price crosses the Supertrend line.

---

## T

**Target** — A predefined price level at which a profitable trade is automatically closed to lock in gains. Each signal includes a suggested target. Example: A LONG signal at 22,500 might have a target at 22,700, meaning if the price rises to 22,700, the position is closed with a 200-point profit.

**Tick Size** — The minimum price movement allowed for an instrument on the exchange. For most NSE F&O instruments, tick size is ₹0.05. Orders cannot be placed at prices that are not multiples of the tick size.

**Trading Mode** — Whether the platform is operating in Paper mode (simulated, no real money) or Live mode (real orders, real money). Shown in the top status bar at all times. Changed from the Settings or Broker page.

---

## U

**Universe** — The list of instruments that StockSignalAnalyzer actively monitors and scans. Managed on the Universe page. You can add or remove instruments from the universe. Only instruments in the universe are considered for signal generation.

**Unrealized PnL** — The theoretical profit or loss on a position that is still open. It changes continuously as the market price moves. It is not actual profit or loss until the position is closed. Shown in real time on the Positions page.

---

## V

**VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price)** — The average price at which an instrument has traded throughout the day, weighted by volume. Price trading above VWAP generally indicates bullish intraday momentum; below VWAP indicates bearish momentum. The platform's VWAP Momentum strategy uses price-to-VWAP relationships to generate signals.

---

## W

**WebSocket** — A real-time communication technology that keeps your browser continuously connected to the platform's server. It powers the live price updates, signal notifications, order status changes, and kill switch status you see without needing to manually refresh the page. If the WebSocket connection drops, real-time updates will stop. Check System Health if prices or positions stop updating.

**Win Rate** — The percentage of completed trades that were profitable. A win rate of 60% means 60 out of every 100 closed trades made money. Win rate alone does not determine profitability — what matters is the ratio of average wins to average losses. A 40% win rate can still be profitable if winners are significantly larger than losers.

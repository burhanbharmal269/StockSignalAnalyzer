# AD-P13-01: Phase 13 Correlation Check Is a Correlation-Adjusted Net-Delta Check

**Status:** Accepted  
**Date:** 2026-06-13  
**Phase:** 13 — Risk Engine  
**Applies to:** `check_correlation` in `src/core/domain/risk/risk_limit_checker.py`

---

## Context

Phase 13 implements 15 pre-trade risk checks (Checks 1–15). Check 9 (Correlation) was
designed to catch risk from holding concentrated positions in correlated instruments — for
example, a large NIFTY long combined with a new BANKNIFTY trade in the same direction.

The original implementation used a **GROSS formulation**:
```
correlated_exposure = (|portfolio.net_delta| + |new_delta_abs|) × max_rho
```

This formulation treated the absolute value of both the existing portfolio delta and the
new position delta, then scaled by the maximum pairwise correlation. The intent was to
detect cases where the total correlated magnitude exceeded the limit, regardless of
direction.

## Problem

The GROSS formulation incorrectly rejected **risk-reducing trades**. A portfolio that was
heavily short (e.g., `net_delta = −2,480`) near the −2,500 limit could not add a LONG
CALL (delta = +25) even though that trade **reduces** the portfolio's directional exposure
from −2,480 to −2,455.

- Check 8 (NetDelta) correctly passes this trade: `|−2,480 + 25| = 2,455 < 2,500`.
- Check 9 (Correlation, GROSS) incorrectly rejects it: `(2,480 + 25) × 1.0 = 2,505 > 2,500`.

The GROSS formula added a spurious rejection case with no corresponding risk benefit.

## Decision

Replace the GROSS formula with a **signed, correlation-adjusted projected net-delta** formula:

```
direction_sign           = +1.0 (LONG) or −1.0 (SHORT)
new_delta_signed         = option_delta × lot_size × direction_sign   [OPTION]
                         = lot_size × direction_sign                   [FUTURE]
corr_adjusted_portfolio  = portfolio.net_delta × max_rho
effective_exposure       = corr_adjusted_portfolio + new_delta_signed
correlated_exposure      = |effective_exposure|
passed                   = correlated_exposure < max_net_delta
```

## Consequences

### What Check 9 now provides

**Same underlying (max_rho = 1.0):**  
Check 9 is identical to Check 8. A risk-reducing opposing-direction trade that passes
Check 8 will also pass Check 9. No false rejections.

**Cross-instrument (max_rho < 1.0):**  
Check 9 is **more lenient** than Check 8, granting a discount proportional to `(1 − max_rho)`.
A NIFTY trade against a BANKNIFTY-heavy portfolio at rho = 0.85 sees only 85% of the
portfolio delta as effective correlated exposure. This is both correct and desirable.

**Matrix cache miss (max_rho = 1.0 via CONSERVATIVE_DEFAULT):**  
Check 9 collapses to Check 8. No false rejections; the check is not net-additive in the
miss case but is safe.

### What Check 9 does NOT provide under this design

Check 9 cannot measure **per-underlying gross correlated exposure** — for example, a
portfolio that is simultaneously long 2,000 delta in NIFTY and short 2,000 delta in BANKNIFTY
(net ≈ 0) carries significant gross exposure if the instruments are correlated. This gross
exposure check would require per-underlying delta breakdown, which is not present in the
current `PortfolioState` schema (only `net_delta` is available).

## Deferred Work

Per-underlying correlated gross exposure analysis requires:
1. `PortfolioState.delta_per_underlying: dict[str, float]` — signed delta sum per underlying.
2. A revised Check 9 formula that iterates over the per-underlying breakdown.

This enhancement is **deferred to Phase 2** (post-live enhancements). It does not affect
the correctness of the current system — the current net-delta and Check 8 already enforce
the aggregate limit. Phase 2 will add granularity.

## Alternatives Considered

**Keep GROSS formula with directional exclusion:** Reject the GROSS result only when the
new trade increases `|net_delta|`. Rejected — this requires comparing pre-trade and
post-trade net delta inside Check 9, duplicating Check 8's computation. Separation of
concerns is better served by a clean signed formula.

**Remove Check 9 entirely until Phase 2:** Rejected — the correlation discount for
cross-instrument trades provides genuine value even without per-underlying breakdown.
A cross-instrument BANKNIFTY trade deserves an 85% discount when the portfolio delta
is NIFTY-dominated.
